# Certification Exam Prep Books – Free Samples & Selected Free Editions

Certification exam prep books, free samples, and selected free editions for Cloud & IT, Securities, Insurance, Accounting, Finance, and Project Management.

This repository is maintained by **Tokenizer Inc. (Mastery Exam Prep)** as a public access point for:

- Free “lite” editions (partial content) of paid books  
- Selected full free editions released by the publisher  
- Errata, updates, and reading guides

Each book or sample links back to its official product page for readers who want the complete edition.

---

## What You’ll Find Here

This repo is organized by **domain** and then by **exam or topic**. A typical structure:

- `cloud-it/` – AWS, Azure, GCP, CompTIA, HashiCorp, Databricks, etc.
- `securities/` – SIE, Series 7, 63, 65, 66, and related licensing exams
- `insurance/` – LLQP and other insurance licensing exams
- `accounting/` – CPA, financial reporting, audit, etc.
- `finance/` – Investment, wealth management, and related certifications
- `project-management/` – PMP and related PM certifications

Inside each directory you’ll typically see:

- A **`README.md`** describing the specific book/exam
- One or more **sample chapters** (usually 15–20% of the full book)
- In some cases, a **complete free edition** (clearly labeled as such)

> **Note:** File layouts may evolve over time as new books and editions are added.

---

## How to Use These Materials

1. **Browse by domain**  
   Drill into the folder that matches your interest (e.g., `cloud-it/aws`, `securities/sie`, etc.).

2. **Read samples locally or online**  
   - View Markdown directly on GitHub, or  
   - Clone the repo and read locally in your favorite editor.

3. **Upgrade to the full edition (optional)**  
   Each sample includes a link to the corresponding full book (print, ebook, or app) so you can continue with the complete set of practice questions and explanations if you find it helpful.

4. **Report issues or typos**  
   If you spot an error, open a GitHub Issue on this repo with:
   - Book / exam name  
   - Chapter / section  
   - The problematic text  
   - Your suggested correction (if any)

This helps keep all editions (free and paid) accurate and up to date.
